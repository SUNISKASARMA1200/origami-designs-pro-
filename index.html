Interesting Facts About Camels:

  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  • When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.
  • Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.

Interesting Facts About Pigeons:

  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • There are over 300 different species of pigeons that can be found throughout the world

Interesting Facts About Chameleons:

  • Chameleons are a very unique branch of the lizard group of reptiles.
  • Chameleons live in warm temperatures.
  • There are around 160 species of chameleon.

Interesting Facts About Teddies:

  • The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  • The first toy bear is believed to be made by German toy maker Margarete Steiff in 1902 that was a jointed, stuffed one.

Interesting Facts About Cicadas:

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • Most have red-orange eyes.
  • Their short adult life is not unusual for bugs.

Interesting Facts About Pandas:

  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  • Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
